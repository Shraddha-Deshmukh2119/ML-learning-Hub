What are Neural Networks?

Neural Networks are computational models inspired by the structure and function of biological neural networks in animal brains. They consist of artificial neurons (nodes) connected together to form a network capable of learning complex patterns.

A neural network typically consists of:

  • Input Layer: Receives the initial data (features)
  • Hidden Layers: Intermediate layers where most computation happens
  • Output Layer: Produces the final prediction or classification

Each connection between neurons has a weight, which adjusts as the network learns. The learning process involves:

  1. Forward propagation - inputs are passed through the network
  2. Calculating loss - comparing output with expected result
  3. Backpropagation - adjusting weights to minimize loss

Types of Neural Networks:

  • Feedforward Neural Networks
  • Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs)
  • Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs)
  • Long Short-Term Memory Networks (LSTMs)
  • Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs)
  • Transformers

Applications:

  • Image and video recognition
  • Natural language processing
  • Speech recognition
  • Medical diagnosis
  • Game playing (e.g., AlphaGo)
  • Autonomous vehicles
